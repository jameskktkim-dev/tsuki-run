# Tsuki Run Design Language

## North Star

Tsuki Run should feel like opening a notebook,
not launching an app.

---

## Brand

A quiet notebook for runners.

---

## Emotion

Quiet

Intentional

Reflective

Gentle

Timeless

Breathing

---

## Product

We encourage planning.

We encourage reflection.

We never judge.

We never compete.

---

## Visual Language

Paper

Ink

Editorial

Natural

Whitespace

---

## Typography

Elegant.

Thin.

Editorial.

Readable.

The logo should feel handwritten,
not decorative.

---

## Layout

Whitespace is a feature.

One focus per screen.

Less is more.

Remove before adding.

---

## Color

Paper

Ink

Muted

Accent only when necessary.

---

## Borders

Very thin.

Dashed where appropriate.

Never heavy.

---

## Motion

Almost invisible.

Fade.

Opacity.

Never bounce.

Never flashy.

---

## Calendar

The calendar is the heart of Tsuki Run.

Plan ○

Completed ●

The calendar should feel like paper.

---

## Voice

Quiet.

Warm.

Thoughtful.

Never motivational.

Never pushy.

Never judging.

---

## Things We Never Do

Gamification.

Leaderboards.

Badges.

Bright gradients.

Heavy shadows.

Crowded dashboards.

Too many buttons.

Too many colors.

---

## Design Test

Before adding a feature, ask:

Does it support reflection?

Does it make the interface quieter?

Does it respect whitespace?

Does it encourage intentional running?

Would this still make sense on paper?

Can we remove something instead?

---

## Design Philosophy

The interface should quietly fade into the background.

The runner and their journal should always be the focus.

---

## Success

Users should remember what they wrote,
not what the interface looked like.
